Song Jinwu vs Cha-In — sustain or pure burst?

Song Jinwu floods the map with autonomous shadows that shred waves while protecting him. Cha-In compresses power into short, flashy windows that delete bosses if you line up buffs correctly. Both are accessible Mythics, but they reward very different habits.

Key differences at a glance

Metric Song Jinwu Cha-In
Rarity Mythic (standard pool) Mythic (event reruns)
Playstyle Summoner sustain / lane control Burst striker / time attack
Damage curve Steady pressure from clones High spikes every rotation
Economy demand High — needs summon upgrades Medium — thrives with base kit
Ease of use Medium (positioning matters) High (plug-and-play)
Best content Endless, defensive raids Boss rush, timed events
Support reliance Low — summons soak hits High — needs buffs & shields

Deep dive analysis

Song Jinwu plays like a one-man squad. Each clone inherits a portion of his stats, zones enemies, and buys time for supports to rotate buffs. He excels when you need to cover multiple spawn points or lack durable front-liners. The downside is cost: upgrading clones and summon durations eats materials quickly.

Cha-In is the go-to option for players who want immediacy. Her wind slashes delete priority targets and clear waves long before they threaten your base. Because she performs well without heavy investments, she is a favorite for alt accounts and event speedruns. However, she is fragile and requires shields or crowd control to keep her burst window uninterrupted.

Choose Jinwu if you prefer strategic, methodical clears or often play solo. Choose Cha-In if you already have supporting pieces and crave faster farming or raid times. Owning both lets you swap based on the week's content.

Actionable takeaways

  • Pick Song Jinwu if you struggle with survival or need autonomous damage on multiple lanes.
  • Pick Cha-In if you enjoy aggressive gameplay and already invested in buffers like Sprintwagon.
  • Running both? Use Jinwu for Endless or defensive slots and deploy Cha-In for bosses or timed challenges.

Frequently asked questions

Who should midgame players build first?

Song Jinwu provides the most safety because his summons keep lanes busy even when you reposition. Build Cha-In first only if you already have reliable shields and buffers.

Does Song Jinwu need micromanagement?

Minimal. Move him between lanes after each wave to refresh summon positions and you are good to go.

What boosts Cha-In the most?

Combine Sprintwagon or Song Jinwu (as a support) for damage amplification with Alligator or Norutu for shields so she can finish her burst loop.
