Sprintwagon vs Alligator — damage or protection?

Sprintwagon supercharges any DPS with attack and crit buffs, while Alligator keeps carries alive with shields and massive slows. Most rosters eventually want both, but prioritizing the right support early saves gems and pity.

Key differences at a glance

Metric Sprintwagon Alligator
Primary buff Attack, crit, and haste aura Shield, regen, and AoE slow
Cooldown cycle Short — constant uptime Medium — timed shields
Scaling Scales with carry crit stats Scales with Alligator HP
Survivability Low — protect her High — built to frontline
Best partners Burst DPS like Gojo, Cha-In Fragile carries like Sukuna, Luffo
Resource cost Low — skill levels only Medium — shield upgrades matter
Playstyle Set-and-forget aura Active positioning for shields

Deep dive analysis

Sprintwagon is the cheapest way to supercharge any damage dealer. Drop her behind your carry and enjoy constant attack, crit, and haste buffs. She requires minimal management, so she fits every team from early story to late raids. The catch is durability—she needs shields or careful placement.

Alligator acts like a budget paladin. She throws shields, applies a massive AoE slow, and stabilizes runs where your carry would otherwise get clipped by stray shots. Her damage is negligible, but keeping her on rotation dramatically reduces wipe risk, especially in Endless.

If your team already clears waves but dies randomly, Alligator plugs the gap. If your runs time out due to lack of DPS, Sprintwagon is the answer. Eventually you will run both—one for offense, one for defense.

Actionable takeaways

  • Prioritize Sprintwagon when you need faster clears or want to buff glass-cannon carries.
  • Prioritize Alligator when survivability is your choke point.
  • Running both covers every situation: Sprintwagon boosts damage while Alligator keeps the team alive.

Frequently asked questions

Which support should I level first?

Sprintwagon if damage is lacking, Alligator if your carries keep dying.

Can I run both on the same team?

Yes, and most late-game teams do. Just make sure you have enough deployment slots.

Are they still useful once I get Mythic supports?

Absolutely. Their kit simplicity and low cost keep them relevant even after you unlock premium options.
